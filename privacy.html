chepherd privacy

version 1.0 · effective 2026-05-24 · contact: privacy@chepherd.org

SHORT VERSION chepherd is a developer tool. We don't sell your data. The default transport between your bastion and your device is peer-to-peer WebRTC — the chepherd relay only sees the signaling handshake. Your data is your data.

1. What chepherd is

chepherd is a TUI + web/iOS/Android dashboard that supervises AI-coding agents you run in tmux sessions on your own machines. The TUI runs locally. The mobile + web clients connect to the local daemon via the optional chepherd-rc remote-control subsystem.

2. What we collect

When you sign in to the remote-control mobile/web client, the OpenOva identity provider issues OAuth2 tokens that authenticate you to the chepherd relay. The relay stores:

  • your user identifier (email address from your IdP claim);
  • the set of bastions you've registered + their hostname/version;
  • device push tokens you opt-in to register (APNs / FCM);
  • connection-state counters for operational telemetry.

The relay does not store the contents of your coding sessions, the verdicts the supervisor emits, or any log/state frame exchanged between your bastion and your device.

3. What chepherd-rc routes through the relay

P2P mode (default): the relay sees SDP offers + ICE candidates for NAT traversal but never decrypts the DataChannel payload — that traffic is DTLS-encrypted directly between your bastion and your device.

Relayed mode (opt-in fallback): when WebRTC NAT traversal fails (symmetric NAT, corporate proxies), you may choose to route the data plane through the relay's WebSocket multiplexer. The relay CAN see those frames in plaintext-at-app over its TLS connection. This trade-off is explicit and opt-in.

4. Telemetry

The relay + web client optionally report error events to Sentry and trace spans to OpenTelemetry collectors. These are env-gated: deployments without Sentry/OTel credentials send nothing. When configured, authorization headers + query strings are stripped from all reports.

5. Where data lives

The chepherd relay is hosted on OpenOva infrastructure (Hetzner, EU region). Push notification dispatch goes through APNs (Apple) and FCM (Google) when you opt in; their respective privacy terms apply for the delivery leg only.

6. Your rights

You can:

  • delete your account by running chepherd rc disable on every registered bastion + signing out of all devices;
  • request a copy or deletion of your stored data by emailing privacy@chepherd.org;
  • self-host the relay entirely (the chepherd-relay source is MIT-licensed) — in that case you control your own data plane and this privacy policy doesn't apply.

7. Open source

All chepherd code is public: github.com/chepherd/chepherd, chepherd-relay, chepherd-rc-web, chepherd-rc-ios, chepherd-rc-android. Verify the contract by reading the code.

8. Changes

Material changes will be announced via release notes on the individual repos + a notice on this page. Last-changed timestamps live in the git history of privacy.html.
